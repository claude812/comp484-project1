What is rugby

Rugby is a similar sport to American football without pads. There are some key differences though. In rugby, you can only pass the ball backwards and if you drop it forward or pass it forward and manage to regather the ball it results in the opposition getting something called a scrum.This is where the forwards who are the players with the numbers 1-8 come together and push the other teams forwards backwards to try to get the ball to their side if defending or keep it at their side if attacking.Another difference is the ball is still live after a tackle, after a tackle is made a ruck is formed where the team with the ball try keep it till the scrum-half the player with a 9 on his back can pass it to a teammate. The defending team can try to steal the ball while this is happening but must stay on their feet if attempting to steal the ball. there is also just one team they don't have an offence and defence team and then the final difference is they call a touch-down a try, and they have to touch the ball down for it to count and then from 10+ yards away from the end zone they can attempt to kick a 2 point conversion in line with where the ball was placed so teams have an easier kick if they place it down closer to the posts.

What is the six nations

The six nations began as the four nations in 1883. The founding nations were Ireland, England, Scotland and Wales. Then in 1910 France joined the competition and it became the five nations. It would stay as the five nations for another 90 years untilItaly joined in the year 2000 to make it what it is known as today. Since then there have been talks of Georgia joining and fans wanting a religation promotion system with the European championship but for now it seems like it is going to stay as the six nations without any promotion or religation. So in short the six nations in a competition where the UK, Ireland, Italy and France compete against each other every year. England currently are by far the most successful team having won the tournament the most after having a very dominant era during the five nations. Recently it has been Wales, Ireland and France that have been the most dominant in the last ten years but unfortunately for Wales about five years ago they had a disastrous fall from grace. Due to the end of a golden generation and lack of money they went from winning every single game in the tournament one season to consistently being bottom two in the tournament table. Although this year Ireland have looked much weaker than normal and more in a transition period then then dominance era they are still a strong that doesnt look to be in danger of having the fall Wales did especially with the young talent getting brought up into the team like 22 year old Edwin Edogbo for example
